# 04 — Outreach

The final stage turns a high-ranking, well-understood company into **draft
emails a human can review and send**. Outreach leans on everything earlier in
the pipeline: the named decision-makers from enrichment, and the fit, caveats and
angle seed from scoring.

## What does the outreach

Outreach is generated by a Kindling Pipeline with the role `draft_outreach`
(pipeline `kindling-draft-outreach`). It's given the company's full enriched
profile and our active positioning, and asked to write outreach grounded in the
specific evidence we hold about that firm — not a generic template.

## Three styles, every time

For each company the pipeline produces **three variant drafts** in different
voices, so you can pick the tone that suits the firm:

| Style id                 | Label             | When it fits                                                        |
|--------------------------|-------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `direct_practical`       | Direct practical  | Concise, operational, owner-to-owner. Leads with a concrete benefit.|
| `consultative_problem_led`| Consultative     | Opens with a genuine question, invites a light conversation.         |
| `local_plainspoken`      | Local plain-spoken| Plain, low-jargon, WA-to-WA. Good for independent, local firms.      |

Each draft contains the full set of pieces you need to actually send it:

- **Subject line** and **opening line** (the hook).
- The **body** of the email.
- A **call to action** — usually a low-pressure offer of a short 20-minute call.
- A **rationale** explaining why this angle was chosen (it references the score,
  confidence and any triggers).
- A **confidence** score for the draft.

## Grounded in the score, honest about gaps

The drafts are written to match what scoring found:

- For **medium-fit** firms with **no live trigger**, the language stays modest
  and question-led — we float a hypothesis (e.g. "a lot of the big calls
  probably still route through you") rather than claiming we know their problem.
- For firms with a real trigger (succession, rapid hiring, multi-office growth),
  the outreach can lean into it more directly.

## Warnings and the draft-only rule

Every variant carries a **warnings** list — and the most important warning is
always the same: these are **drafts only, do not send automatically**. Other
warnings flag things a person must fix before sending, such as:

- The greeting is generic — verify and address the correct decision-maker.
- Owner-dependency or growth strain is **inferred**, not stated by the firm.
- Fit is borderline, or only a generic `info@`/`hello@` address is known.

In other words, the pipeline gets you 90% of the way to a thoughtful, personal
email — but a human always makes the final call on accuracy and timing before it
goes out.

## Where it lands

Generating outreach moves a company toward the `outreach_ready` / `outreach`
ring. The drafts are stored against the company for review, ready for a person to
choose a variant, personalise it, and send.
