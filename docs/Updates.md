## 1. Service Offering Corrections

The current Adapt / Lumia service definition is broadly accurate, but needs these corrections:

- The design phase should not say it helps the "core team". It primarily helps the ownership team see the business clearly.
- The design phase clarifies roles and decision rights at the ownership-team level, while exposing gaps across the broader business.
- Add owner alignment as a core benefit.
- The vision is the destination; the Journey Progress is the practical action plan.
- Handover design and critical knowledge capture are only early-stage outputs during the design phase.
- Keep Adapt / Lumia positioned as coaching plus platform, not coaching alone and not software alone.
- Add that Adapt owns its intellectual property and owns the technology platform it runs on.
- Add that the IP and technology are researched, developed, owned, and operated in Australia.

Preferred service framing:

Adapt helps privately owned and family SMEs reduce owner dependency, align owners and leaders, clarify decision rights, capture critical knowledge, build an operating rhythm, and create more options to scale, exit, step back, or transition.

## 2. ICP / Targeting Corrections

The ICP is mostly accepted.

Strong-fit targets:

- Family SMEs and privately owned SMEs.
- Founder-led, partner-led, or owner-managed businesses.
- Professional, scientific, technical, engineering, construction, advisory, accounting, legal, and recruitment-type firms.
- Businesses with enough complexity to benefit from workshops, delegation, decision-right clarity, leadership cadence, and platform capture.

Geography:

Disqualifiers:

- Sole practitioners.

- Very early-stage startups.

- Businesses unwilling to include the owner or leadership group.
- Businesses without credible owner-dependency, alignment, scaling, succession, or handover pressure.
- Businesses with no website should probably not be treated as sufficiently enriched for outreach.

## 5. Positioning / Outreach Feedback

This is the most important correction.

The current outreach often starts with strong research context, but then moves too quickly into a pitch. The opening line can be powerful when it proves the business was actually researched, but the message should then ask permission to give value or invite a low-friction diagnostic step.

Outreach should not lead with Lumia Design as the first step. It should lead with a free diagnostic / diagnostic workshop / self-diagnostic.

Preferred first-contact goal:

- A reply.
- A call.
- Completion of the diagnostic.
- Permission to share something useful.

Do not try to sell the full engagement in the first email.

## 6. Preferred Outreach Structure

Use this structure:

1. We researched your business and understand roughly where it is.
2. From our experience, businesses like yours often have an opportunity to increase enterprise value, reduce owner dependency, create more owner freedom, and open up scale / exit / transition options.
3. We would like to show you how or share something useful.
4. A good first step is the free diagnostic. It gives you insight and lets you assess whether Adapt is credible.

Keep the message short, plain-spoken, and low-friction.

Avoid defensive AI-style contrast lines such as:

- "Not generic coaching and not just software."
- "We are not a technology implementation firm."

Those points may be true, but they read like justification. The email should speak from authority rather than defending the category.

## 7. Core Positioning Angle

The strongest hook should be business worth / business value.

Gabe's strategic view is that owners may agree with alignment, cadence, handover, leadership, and knowledge-capture language but still not act because they are busy. The value angle is more likely to stop them:

- Your business may be worth less than it should be.
- Owner dependency creates a discount in the business.
- The business becomes more valuable when it can run without everything routing through the owner.
- Reducing reliance on the owner can increase enterprise value, create owner freedom, and improve exit or transition options.

This value / worth angle should become the lead positioning hypothesis for outreach.

## 8. Example Outreach Direction

Use this as direction, not final copy:

Hi [Name],

We have been looking at [Company] and the way the business appears to have grown around specialist client relationships, senior people, and founder / partner knowledge.

In businesses like this, a lot of value can sit inside a few heads. When that happens, the business can be more dependent on key people than the owner wants, and that usually affects freedom, scale options, and eventual enterprise value.

Adapt helps privately owned Australian firms reduce that dependency and make the business more valuable without losing what made it work in the first place.

A simple first step is our free diagnostic. It will give you a clear read on the biggest dependency and value gaps in the business, and it is also an easy way to decide whether we are credible.

Would it be useful if I sent that through?

## 9. Outreach Voice Rules

The outreach should sound like Gabe and Adam, not like a generic AI-generated sales email.

Voice principles:

- Short.
- Plain-spoken.
- Specific to the company.
- Confident but not pitchy.
- Research-led, but not verbose.
- Value-first, not product-first.
- Low-friction ask.
- Avoid jargon unless it is normal owner language.
- Avoid long justification paragraphs.

The "Plain Spoken" variant was closest to the preferred direction, but all variants should be shortened and made less pitch-forward.

Recommended follow-up input:

- Build a Gabe / Adam writing voice guide from their real written examples.
- Add examples of actual cold emails they have sent, if available.
