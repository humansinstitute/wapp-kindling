# Part A — ADAPT Service Offering Inside Kindling

## Summary

This section is about how the ADAPT service offering should be represented inside Kindling, based on the current Adapt site.

The service profile Kindling uses for ADAPT should lead with owner independence, enterprise value, and the cost of owner dependency. It should not lead with generic business coaching, succession planning, software, or AI-assisted prospecting.

## Source-Of-Truth Positioning

ADAPT helps established privately held Australian SME owners reduce owner-dependence, lift leadership capability, and build a business that can run, grow, transition, sell, or hand over without the owner in every decision.

The site’s sharper commercial idea:

> A business is worth more when it no longer depends on the owner.

Kindling should store this as the central positioning hypothesis for ADAPT outreach:

- Owner dependency creates a discount on business value.
- Key-person risk reduces buyer or successor confidence.
- Knowledge, relationships, decisions, rhythm, and culture trapped in the owner suppress enterprise value.
- ADAPT designs that dependency out through coaching plus the Lumia platform.
- The owner gains options: scale, sell, step back, transition, or pass the business on.

## Core Offer To Represent

Kindling should model ADAPT as one connected program, not separate coaching and software.

### Owner Independence Review

Complimentary 45-minute review.

Purpose: give the owner a private read on where the business still depends on them and what that dependence is quietly costing its value.

Use when outreach needs a low-friction human next step.

### Owner Independence Diagnostic

Free online diagnostic.

Purpose: show the owner their highest-risk dependency area and the first move to make.

Use when outreach should invite self-assessment before a conversation.

### Design

Phase one of Adapt Lumia.

Purpose: align owners, see where the business still leans on them, document the vision and priorities, map dependency, and create the roadmap to independence.

Price signal: begins at $15,000 and scales up for larger ownership teams.

### Build

Phase two of Adapt Lumia.

Purpose: execute the roadmap, move real decisions into the leadership team, capture how the business runs, and build the cadence, accountability, culture, and systems that let the business hold without the owner.

Price signal: begins at $4,500 a month and typically scales up to around $10,000 a month.

### Lumia Platform

Included from day one.

Purpose: hold roles, vision, rhythm, culture, progress, succession path, decisions, and knowledge in one place so the independence built through coaching stays in the business.

## Ideal Customer Profile

Prioritise:

- Established privately held Australian SMEs.
- Roughly 20 to 200+ people.
- Typically $2M to $100M+ revenue.
- Owner-led, founder-led, family-owned, partner-led, or privately owned.
- Businesses owner-run for ten years or more.
- Owners weighing succession, sale, scale, semi-exit, stepping back, or next-generation handover.
- Professional, technical, engineering, construction, advisory, IT, accounting, legal, recruitment, health, property, and expert-led firms.

Deprioritise:

- Very small businesses without a leadership group.
- Businesses without visible owner involvement.
- Startups primarily chasing venture scale.
- Organisations seeking generic AI, marketing, CRM, or productivity consulting.

## Pain And Signal Model

Kindling scoring should look for evidence of:

- Founder or owner centrality.
- Succession, sale, handover, or generational transition pressure.
- Growth strain.
- Leadership team expansion or role confusion.
- Professionalising after years of informal systems.
- Knowledge concentrated in owners or a few senior people.
- Culture depending on the owner being in the room.
- Hiring, acquisition, expansion, new leadership appointments, or restructure.
- Advisory/accounting/legal/business-sale signals suggesting value, exit, or continuity questions.

The strongest outreach angles should connect those signals to business worth:

- "What is the business worth without you?"
- "Where is owner dependency still discounting value?"
- "Could a buyer or successor trust this business to perform without you?"
- "What needs to move from your head into your people and systems?"
- "Is the business an asset you own, or still a job you carry?"

## Outreach Rules

First contact should not try to sell the full program. It should usually point to the Owner Independence Diagnostic or the confidential Owner Independence Review.

Use plain owner language:

- "worth more when you step back"
- "founder-dependency discount"
- "key-person risk"
- "enterprise value"
- "business that can run without you"
- "turn the business you run into the asset you own"
- "leaders who carry real decisions"
- "knowledge out of your head and into the business"

Avoid top-of-funnel language that makes ADAPT sound like:

- generic business coaching
- generic succession planning
- standalone software
- AI implementation
- a leadership workshop vendor
- a lead-generation or marketing service

## Recommended Kindling Profile Statement

> ADAPT helps established privately held Australian SME owners build a business worth more without them at the centre. Through Adapt Lumia, a coaching-plus-platform program, ADAPT designs out owner dependency, moves real decisions into the leadership team, captures how the business runs, and builds the rhythm, culture, and accountability that protect enterprise value.

## Recommended First-Step CTA

Default CTA:

> Take the Owner Independence Diagnostic.

Higher-trust CTA:

> Book a confidential Owner Independence Review.

Use Design or Build only after the prospect has shown owner-dependency, succession, value, sale, or leadership-readiness fit.

# Part B — Suggested ADAPT Service Offering Updates for Kindling

**Source:** review transcript between Gabe and Adam on the Kindling Adapt / Lumia workflow.

**Purpose:** this is not a patch to the current Kindling service-offering text. The current generated service offering is not the right source of truth. This section captures the service-offering model Gabe and Adam were actually describing, so Kindling can be updated with a better ADAPT profile alongside specific data, scoring, and outreach updates.

## Core Positioning

ADAPT should be understood as a coaching-plus-platform business for privately owned Australian SMEs, especially businesses where value, decision making, knowledge, and momentum still route through the owner, founder, family, or partner group.

The core promise is not simply "succession planning", "business coaching", or "software implementation". ADAPT helps owners make the business more valuable and less dependent on them by aligning ownership, clarifying priorities and decision rights, capturing knowledge, designing handover pathways, and embedding a practical operating rhythm.

The highest-leverage positioning angle is **business value / business worth**:

- Owner dependency creates a discount in the business.
- A business that relies heavily on the owner is worth less than it could be.
- A business that can keep working, growing, and transitioning without everything routing through the owner has more enterprise value.
- The owner gains more options: scale, exit, step back, transition, or pass the business on.

Kindling should treat this value / worth angle as the **lead strategic hypothesis** for ADAPT outreach and positioning.

## Service Offering Taxonomy

Kindling should model ADAPT as having at least four connected service-offering layers:

1. ADAPT overall market offer.
2. Free diagnostic / diagnostic workshop as the first conversion step.
3. Lumia Design as the diagnostic and design phase.
4. Lumia Build as the longer implementation and operating-rhythm phase.

The offers should be connected, but not collapsed into one generic coaching proposition.

### 1. ADAPT Overall Offer

**What It Is**

ADAPT combines experienced SME ownership coaching with a proprietary platform that captures and connects the work required to reduce owner dependency, align leadership, protect culture, clarify priorities, and build a business that can scale or transition without chaos.

**Who It Is For**

Best-fit businesses:

- Family SMEs.
- Privately owned SMEs.
- Founder-led, owner-led, partner-led, or owner-managed firms.
- Professional, technical, engineering, construction, advisory, accounting, legal, recruitment, and similar expert-led firms.
- Businesses with enough complexity that informal systems, owner memory, or ad hoc leadership cadence are starting to strain.
- Businesses where the owner or partner group is still the bottleneck.

**Primary Problems**

ADAPT addresses:

- Owner dependency.
- Misalignment between owners, partners, or leadership.
- Unclear priorities.
- Unclear roles, accountability, and decision rights.
- Knowledge trapped in key people.
- Weak leadership cadence.
- Handover risk.
- Succession, exit, growth, or transition pressure.
- Business value being suppressed because the business depends too heavily on the owner.

**Primary Outcomes**

ADAPT should be positioned around:

- Clearer owner alignment.
- Clearer decisions and priorities.
- Reduced owner dependency.
- Better role and decision-right clarity.
- Captured critical knowledge.
- Stronger leadership cadence and accountability.
- A protected culture during growth or transition.
- More valuable business asset.
- More owner freedom.
- More options to scale, exit, step back, or transition.

### 2. Free Diagnostic / Diagnostic Workshop

**What It Is**

The diagnostic should be the default first step in outreach. It should be low-friction and framed as a way for the prospect to get value before committing to anything larger.

The transcript refers to this as a "diagnostic workshop" and also discusses a free diagnostic or self-diagnostic. **Kindling should not assume the exact delivery mechanics** until Gabe/Adam confirm whether it is self-serve, workshop-led, call-led, or a hybrid.

**Why It Matters**

Gabe and Adam were clear that first outreach should not try to sell the full design phase. The goal of first contact is a reply, a call, or diagnostic completion.

The diagnostic is useful because it:

- Gives the owner a quick read on where dependency and value gaps exist.
- Lets the prospect assess whether ADAPT is credible.
- Reduces friction compared with asking for a larger engagement.
- Creates a natural path into Lumia Design if there is a real fit.

**Suggested Positioning**

The diagnostic should be positioned around:

- "See where owner dependency is reducing business value."
- "Identify the biggest value and handover gaps."
- "Understand what would need to change for the business to run with less reliance on the owner."
- "Use the diagnostic as a simple way to evaluate ADAPT before committing to more."

**Kindling Rule**

Cold outreach should lead to the diagnostic, **not** directly to Lumia Design or a 12-month program.

### 3. Lumia Design

**What It Is**

Lumia Design is the diagnostic and design phase. The transcript references an "up to 60 day" phase with four workshops, coaching, and platform access.

**Critical Correction**

Do **not** describe Lumia Design as helping the "core team" first. Gabe corrected this: it helps the **ownership team** see the business clearly.

The design phase can expose gaps across the whole business, but the primary alignment work happens with the ownership team.

**What It Should Deliver**

Lumia Design should be framed as helping the ownership team:

- See the business clearly.
- Align on vision and priorities.
- Clarify roles and decision rights at ownership level.
- Expose blind spots across the business.
- Identify owner dependency.
- Begin handover thinking.
- Begin critical knowledge capture.
- Create the practical action pathway.

**Language To Use**

Gabe's framing:

- The vision is the destination.
- The Journey Progress is the plan.

Kindling should reflect that the practical action plan is not an abstract strategy document. It is the journey from the current business to the intended destination.

**Benefits To Add**

Add **owner alignment** as a key benefit. Keep:

- Documented vision.
- Sharper diagnosis of owner dependency.
- Role, accountability, and decision-right clarity.
- Early handover design.
- Early critical knowledge capture.
- Practical action plan / Journey Progress.

**Guardrail**

Do not overstate how much handover design and knowledge capture happens in Design. Gabe described these as **early-stage** outputs at this phase.

### 4. Lumia Build

**What It Is**

Lumia Build is the longer implementation phase. The transcript refers to a 12-month coaching plus platform implementation phase.

**What It Should Deliver**

Lumia Build should embed:

- Operating rhythm.
- Leadership cadence.
- Role clarity.
- Delegation.
- Accountability systems.
- Captured business knowledge.
- Professionalised management rhythm.
- Stronger business infrastructure.

**Outcomes**

Build should be positioned as the phase that turns Design into durable operating change. Benefits:

- Leadership team alignment.
- Improved delegation.
- Clearer accountability.
- Captured knowledge and decisions.
- More professional operating rhythm.
- Reduced dependence on the owner.
- Stronger business with more options to scale, transition, or exit.

**Guardrail**

Do not position Build as pure coaching or pure software rollout. It is **coaching plus platform implementation**, grounded in the ADAPT method.

## Differentiators To Add Across All Offers

Kindling should include these differentiators in the ADAPT market profile:

- Human-centric first, technology-enabled second.
- Coaches have owned businesses themselves and understand owner pressure directly.
- ADAPT owns its intellectual property.
- ADAPT owns the platform the IP is built into.
- The IP and platform are researched, developed, owned, and operated in Australia.
- The methodology is grounded in succession and value-protection thinking.
- The platform is a real differentiator because it captures, connects, and keeps work visible.

**Important distinction:** ADAPT is not using third-party IP on a proprietary platform, and it is not using proprietary IP on a third-party platform. The claim Gabe wants captured is that ADAPT **owns both the IP and the technology**.

## Positioning Guardrails

Kindling should avoid framing ADAPT as:

- A generic business coach.
- A generic succession planner.
- A software vendor.
- A technology implementation firm.
- A small-business services provider targeting companies merely because they are small.

Target for owner dependency plus alignment / handover / scaling pressure, **not** generic small-business status.

## Buying Triggers

Kindling should score and position around these triggers:

- Owner starts asking whether the business could run if they stepped away tomorrow.
- Succession, sale, exit, or generational transfer is being considered.
- Business has grown beyond informal systems.
- Leadership team or partner group disagreement.
- Founder, owner, or key partner is carrying too much.
- Growth, hiring, new service lines, or new geographies expose gaps in structure.
- Owner burnout.
- Family business conversation about who will lead next.
- The business is profitable, but still too dependent on the owner to realise full value.

## Outreach Implications

First-contact outreach should be short, plain-spoken, and research-led. Preferred structure:

1. Show that ADAPT has researched the company.
2. Identify the likely dependency / value / key-person risk in simple terms.
3. Explain the upside in owner language: more value, more freedom, more options.
4. Offer the diagnostic as the first step.
5. Ask a low-friction permission question.

Avoid long pitch paragraphs and defensive contrast lines like "not generic coaching and not just software". Those points can remain in the service profile, but should not dominate first-contact copy.

**Example Outreach Direction** (direction only, not final approved copy):

> We have been looking at [Company] and the way the business appears to have grown around specialist client relationships, senior people, and founder / partner knowledge.
>
> In businesses like this, a lot of value can sit inside a few heads. When that happens, the business can be more dependent on key people than the owner wants, and that usually affects freedom, scale options, and eventual enterprise value.
>
> ADAPT helps privately owned Australian firms reduce that dependency and make the business more valuable without losing what made it work in the first place.
>
> A simple first step is the diagnostic. It gives you a clear read on the biggest dependency and value gaps in the business, and it is also an easy way to decide whether we are credible.
>
> Would it be useful if I sent that through?
